Education System

General Information

  • The University offers a wide variety of training services to suit various kinds of persons ranging from pupils uncertain about their aspirations to mature, experienced specialists, scientists, scholars and teachers. Degrees
    The University conducts training generally resulting in the following degrees: Bachelor’s, Engineer’s, Master’s and PhD. In the framework of education in the Russian Federation there exist the following programs:
    • Bachelor’s/ Engineer’s defined as primary education. Persons eligible for these programs must possess a secondary education certificate or a primary or secondary vocational education diploma. Bachelor’s program duration is 4 - 4,5 years, while Engineer’s is 5 - 5,5 years. Upon completing Bachelor’s program, students are certified as a Bachelor and may continue training for a Master’s degree.
    • Master’s defined as the second level of education. Persons eligible for this program must possess a Bachelor’s or Engineer’s degree. Master’s program duration is 2 years. Upon completing the training, students are certified as a Master and may choose to continue training for a PhD.
    • Postgraduate programs (PhD) defined as the third (final) level of tertiary education. Training is conducted according to postdoc programs lasting 3-4 years (depending on the area of research). Upon completing the training, students are to defend their theses and certified as a Doctor of Philosophy.

    Study modes
    All modes of study are available at the university, in particular: full time, blended training including distant training.
    Six-day working week is enforced at MIREA - Russian Technological University. Full-time students study from 9 am to 6 pm, blended learning students study from 6 pm to 9 pm.
    A number of education programs are partly carried out by means of e-training via the internet. Learning materials, student’s guidebooks, tests, etc. are available via e-training provided by the university distant training center.

Base Education System

  • The University training system focuses on broad attraction of leading scientific centers and enterprises of knowledge-intensive branches of science and technology to the educational process.

    At present the University has more than 50 strategic partner enterprises that house base chairs where students major in special disciplines, take traineeship and externship programs, do research for course papers and theses.

    The University training is based on the innovative principle of combination of junior students’ fundamental training, which exceeds standard levels of a typical engineering higher education institution, with senior students’ training in future workplaces. Such training is provided by leading scientists and specialists from major scientific institutes of the Russian Academy of Sciences and industrial enterprises which have become the University strategic partners (base enterprises).
